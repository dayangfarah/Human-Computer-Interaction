## 📖 Course Synopsis
This course will introduce students to human-computer interaction theories and design processes. The emphasis will be on applied user experience (UX) design. The course will present an iterative evaluation centred UX lifecycle and will introduce a broader notion of user experience, including usability, usefulness, and emotional impact. The lifecycle should be viewed as template intended to be instantiated in many ways to match the constraints of a particular development project. The UX lifecycle activities cover contextual inquiry and analysis, requirements extraction, design-informing models, design thinking, ideation, sketching, conceptual design, and formative evaluation.

## 💭 Reflection

### What I Learned
Taking SECV2113 fundamentally changed how I view software engineering by shifting my focus from back-end logic to the actual human experience at the glass. I learned that data-driven engineering means nothing if a system lacks utility, accessibility, or emotional impact. Diving into human cognition and emotional interaction principles taught me how users perceive information, interpret layouts, and navigate digital interfaces under real-world conditions. 

### Challenges & Growth
The greatest area of growth for me was executing the full, iterative user experience (UX) lifecycle. Transitioning from unstructured assumptions to objective user profiling using tools like personas, scenarios, and Hierarchical Task Analysis (HTA) was an intense but valuable methodology upgrade. Building interactive prototypes through successive validation rounds going from fast conceptual sketches and wireframes to deploying and testing functional designs with actual human participants showed me how critical empirical usability engineering is to preventing costly product failures.

### Future Application
As I continue building digital platforms, data repositories, and AI systems, the user-centered paradigms I picked up in this course will serve as a constant compass. I plan to seamlessly embed contextual inquiry, interface mapping, and rigorous heuristic frameworks directly into my development lifecycle. Designing with clean visual psychology (like Gestalt principles) and establishing strong metrics during usability assessments will allow me to deliver production environments that are structurally optimal, universally accessible, and frictionless to use.
